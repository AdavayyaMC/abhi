Research & Analysis

Metabolism & Contemporary Urbanism

An analytical study examining how the Japanese Metabolist movement's principles of flexibility, growth, and adaptability remain relevant in contemporary urban design. Through comparative analysis of Nakagin Capsule Tower and modern modular housing, this research explores how plug-in architecture concepts can address current housing crises.

Key Influences

Kisho Kurokawa Kenzo Tange Metabolism Movement

Light & Shadow in Tadao Ando

A phenomenological investigation of Ando's use of concrete, light, and geometric purity. This study documents how natural light transforms minimal spaces throughout daily and seasonal cycles, examining Church of Light and Water Temple through photography, drawings, and spatial analysis.

Key Influences

Tadao Ando Louis Kahn Japanese Minimalism

About

I am a fourth-year architecture student at the Graduate School of Architecture, Planning and Preservation, passionate about the relationship between spatial design, social equity, and environmental sustainability.

My work explores how architecture can serve as a catalyst for community building and ecological regeneration. Through rigorous research and iterative design processes, I seek to create spaces that are both poetically resonant and pragmatically responsive to contemporary challenges.

Design Philosophy

"Architecture is not merely about shelter—it is about creating frameworks for human experience, moments of beauty within the everyday, and responsible stewardship of our shared environment. Every project is an opportunity to question, refine, and reimagine our relationship with the built world."
